# Style DNA

## One-Line Definition

Pure white, minimal, hand-drawn, lots of white space, restrained, weird, product-sketch feel, handwritten-English feel, clear structure but not like an instruction manual.

It should feel like someone who has spent years doing AI, product, design, and developer tools casually sketched an explanatory drawing on a blank sheet of paper.

## Must Have

- 16:9 horizontal composition for in-body article placement.
- Pure white background: no beige, warm gray, paper texture, gradients, shadows, noise, or vintage-paper feel.
- Black hand-drawn line work as the main element: thin lines, slight wobble, organic rather than mechanical, not vector, no heavy or thick outlines.
- Lots of white space: the subject fills about 40%-60% of the frame, with at least 35% empty space, ideally including one whole quiet area.
- A few handwritten English annotations by default: 5-8 spots max, each ideally 2-6 words. Use another language only when the user requests it.
- One image conveys only a single core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- Let the structure come through naturally; do not write the structure type on the image.

## Color

- Black: main line work, characters, frame lines, structure, primary text, and main objects.
- Red: key annotations, problems, emotional beats, critical reminders, and outcomes.
- Orange: main flow, paths, arrows, automation direction, and movement from A to B.
- Blue: supplementary notes, mental/internal state, system state, second-layer explanation, and AI/assistant/automation hints.

Blue is not required in every image. Keep color restrained: less is better.

## Aesthetic Direction

Weird, creative, interesting, clean and crisp, free-spirited.

Not cute, not childish, not overly complex, not rigid.

## Absolutely Not

- No commercial illustration.
- No PPT infographic.
- No formal flowchart.
- No course or lecture slides.
- No cute cartoon poster.
- No children's illustration.
- No complex architecture diagram.
- No polished flat illustration.
- No techy UI.
- No real app screenshots.
- No busy backgrounds, gradients, shadows, or textures.
- Do not explain every node.
- Do not write a type-label in the top-left like "Workflow", "System Architecture", "Common Pitfalls", or "Roadmap".
- No brand logos, company marks, or readable brand wordmarks on the character or in the scene unless the user explicitly asks.
