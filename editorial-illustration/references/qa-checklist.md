# QA Checklist

Before delivering or accepting a generated image, check:

- 16:9 horizontal composition.
- Clean pure white background.
- No beige, warm gray, paper texture, gradients, shadows, noise, or vintage-paper feel.
- Thin black hand-drawn line work dominates, with slight wobble and an organic product-sketch feel.
- Red/orange/blue accents are sparse.
- Subject fills roughly 40%-60% of the frame.
- At least 35% of the image is empty space, ideally with one whole quiet area.
- The character performs the central action.
- The concept maps to the article's idea, not just a vague mood.
- The image has one clear structure, action, state, or metaphor.
- Labels use the requested language, default English.
- Labels are short and legible: 5-8 spots max, ideally 2-6 words each.
- The image does not write the structure type as a visible label.
- No top-left diagram title.
- No PPT flowchart, consulting infographic, course slide, instruction manual, or architecture diagram style.
- No glossy vector, polished flat illustration, 3D render, or stock illustration look.
- No techy UI or real app screenshot.
- No childish/cute treatment unless the user explicitly asks.
- No company logo, brand wordmark, or readable text on the character's clothing.
- The Tinkerer (or user-supplied IP) keeps its silhouette signature — for The Tinkerer: round head + two upright tufts + plain white coverall + black line work only.
- Enough whitespace for article-body placement.

If two or more checks fail, regenerate rather than explaining the problem away.
