# IP Character

## Default IP: The Tinkerer

Use **The Tinkerer** as the default character unless the user provides a different IP.

The Tinkerer is a deliberately minimal, brandless placeholder figure. Its job is to make the structural rule of this skill possible: *the character must drive the core action of the image, not decorate it*. A simple, recognizable, repeatable silhouette gives the AI a stable anchor across many illustrations, and gives the reader continuity across an article.

### Core visual traits

Translate The Tinkerer into the skill's thin black hand-drawn product-sketch style. The traits below are silhouette rules, not glossy character-design requirements.

- **Head**: a clean round head, slightly oversized relative to the body, drawn as a single thin black line. No facial shading.
- **Eyes**: two small dot eyes, evenly spaced. Calm, attentive expression. No pupils, no eyelashes, no eyebrows by default. Add a small curved brow only when the article concept calls for concentration or surprise.
- **Mouth**: a tiny straight or slightly curved line. Usually neutral. Smile only when the scene genuinely earns it.
- **Hair / antennae**: two short upright strokes on top of the head — like a pair of "stay-awake" tufts or thin antennae. This is the silhouette signature; do not omit them.
- **Body**: small, compact, simply outlined. Loose work overalls or a plain coverall: pure white, no logo, no patch, no readable text, no chest emblem.
- **Hands**: simple mitten-like or three-finger outlined hands. The hands are usually doing something — holding, pulling, balancing, repairing.
- **Feet**: plain rounded shoes, no laces, no brand details.
- **Palette on the figure itself**: black line work only. No colored fill on the body. Color in the scene comes from the red / orange / blue annotation accents around the character, not from costume.

The Tinkerer is intentionally androgynous, age-neutral, and culture-neutral. Do not add gendered hair, jewelry, makeup, glasses, ties, badges, or culturally specific clothing unless the article's topic explicitly requires it.

### Why this design

- The two upright tufts give the silhouette a unique signature, so it reads as "the same character" across an article without needing a face or brand.
- The plain white coverall keeps the scene's color budget for meaning (red / orange / blue annotations), not costume.
- The minimal face avoids cuteness, mascot-poster energy, and emotional overacting.
- No logo, no chest mark, no readable text on the body — so the figure is safe to reuse across any topic without leaking brand context.

## Role In The Image

The Tinkerer must participate in the core action:

- Carrying, sorting, dragging, repairing, balancing, mapping, filtering, inspecting, catching, splitting, stitching, measuring, watering, or negotiating the central metaphor.
- Creating the state change the article is describing.
- Revealing the contradiction, bottleneck, or transformation.
- Acting as the operator inside the idea, not as a presenter outside it.

Do not use The Tinkerer as:

- A passive observer.
- A logo in the corner.
- A decorative sticker.
- A waving figure beside the actual concept.
- A smiling narrator pointing at a diagram.

## Personality

- Curious, patient, slightly mischievous.
- Serious enough to solve the strange task in front of it.
- Competent but sometimes overmatched by the metaphor — that gap is where the article's tension shows.
- Warm without becoming childish, cute-poster-like, or slapstick.

## Brand Treatment

- Keep the figure entirely black line work on white. No filled body color.
- No logos, wordmarks, chest icons, or text on clothing.
- Do not add accessories that imply a specific company, country, profession, or subculture unless the article's topic requires it.
- Do not distort The Tinkerer into a generic monster, bug, robot, or realistic human portrait.
- Do not add extra eyes, teeth, wings, weapons, or unrelated costume elements unless the user explicitly requests a variant.

## Common Generation Corrections

- If the figure appears with colored body fill, regenerate.
- If the head loses the two upright tufts, regenerate — the silhouette signature is gone.
- If a logo, brand mark, or readable word appears on the clothing, regenerate.
- If the face becomes detailed (pupils, eyelashes, blush, full smile lines), regenerate — it has drifted toward cartoon mascot.
- If the figure is standing aside pointing at the concept instead of operating it, regenerate.

## Replacement Rules

If the user supplies a different IP:

- Preserve its required silhouette and brand constraints.
- Keep the same editorial illustration grammar (line weight, color discipline, whitespace, single-structure rule).
- Make the user's IP the active subject of the central metaphor.
- Do not mix The Tinkerer with the user's IP unless asked.
- If the user's IP has its own canonical color, allow that single color on the character; the rest of the color discipline still applies to the scene.
