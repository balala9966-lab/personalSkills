# Storytelling

The default preset for personal essays, reflections, "what I learned" pieces. Combines `scene` Type with `warm` Style — emotional, atmospheric, character-driven.

## Why This Combo

`scene` Type creates the narrative space; `warm` Style fills it with the golden-hour register that makes the reader feel they're sitting across from the author. This is the antithesis of `tech-explainer`.

## Best For

- Personal essays, reflections, growth stories
- "What I learned from X" pieces
- Lifestyle and product-with-feeling writing
- Recommended density: `minimal` to `balanced` — too many scene images dilute emotion

## Tuning Tips

- **Subject**: a single character or pair, in mid-action, never posed
- **Composition**: slightly off-center, golden-hour lighting
- **Avoid**: technical or grid elements — they break the emotional space
- **Faces**: keep abstract or shown from behind / partial — specific faces age the image fast and feel uncanny

## Example Prompt Skeleton

```text
A 16:9 warm scene illustration.

Subject: <one specific moment from the article — what is the protagonist DOING?>

Composition: subject slightly off-center, golden-hour light, environmental detail that grounds the scene (a chair, a window, a notebook).

Style: warm hand-drawn illustration, friendly approachable register, soft warm earth-tone palette (terracotta, gold, cream), no cool colors, gentle texture.

Avoid: grid layouts, technical diagrams, label-heavy compositions, multiple distinct characters competing for attention.
```
