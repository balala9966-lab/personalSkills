## How To Read This

Read along your chosen **Type** column to see which Styles fit. Or read along a **Style** row to see which Types it covers.

For automatic recommendations, see `auto-recommend.md`.

## Cross-Style Rules

These rules sit above the matrix and override it when they apply:

1. **One style per article.** Even when individual illustrations use different Types, keep the Style constant. Mixing `blueprint` and `watercolor` in the same article produces visual whiplash.
2. **Palette can vary by emphasis, not by image.** If image 1 uses `warm` palette, image 4 should not use `cool` for variety. Palettes are part of voice; voice should not flicker.
3. **A `strong` rating beats personal preference.** If your gut says "I want `playful` for a technical infographic", the matrix is telling you to look at `vector-illustration` or `notion` first. Override only with a written reason.
