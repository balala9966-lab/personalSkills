# Editorial

Magazine-style infographic — clean structure with a touch of design polish, the look of a New York Times explainer or Quartz chart.

## Visual Traits

- **Line work**: clean sans-serif typographic feel, moderate weight
- **Fill**: flat solid blocks
- **Background**: white or very light tint
- **Color discipline**: 3-5 colors with strong semantic role (orange for movement, red for emphasis, blue for system state)
- **Typography**: editorial sans, well-kerned, label hierarchy clear
- **Composition**: grid + breathing room; reads like a print spread

## Best For

- `infographic`, `flowchart`, `comparison`, `timeline` — the "newsroom explainer" sweet spot
- Data reporting, process explanations, news-adjacent content

## Avoid For

- `scene` (somewhat ok but better choices exist)
- Whimsical content — editorial seriousness suppresses fun

## Canonical Palette

Pairs best with: `warm` for human-interest topics, `cool` for tech reporting, `mono` for opinion-heavy pieces.

## Generation Prompt Hints

Include:
- "editorial infographic style"
- "clean magazine layout"
- "balanced typography with clear hierarchy"
- "flat solid color blocks"
- "newsroom explainer aesthetic"

Avoid:
- "cartoon", "playful" — too informal
- "photorealistic"
